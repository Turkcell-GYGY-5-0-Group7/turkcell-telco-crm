# 19.2 Verify Automatic Sidecar Injection and mTLS Across All 13 Services; Re-Verify HPA/PDB Resource Accounting with the Sidecar's Footprint

> Sprint: [Sprint 19 - Service Mesh and mTLS](./README.md)
> Feature ID: 19.2
> Subtasks: 19.2.1, 19.2.2, 19.2.3

## Objective

Confirm every one of the 13 services is proxy-injected and communicating over active mTLS, and
re-verify the Sprint 15.3 HPA (`min 2 / max 5 / 75% CPU`) and PDB (`minAvailable: 1`) behavior with the
`linkerd-proxy` sidecar's resource footprint and added pod-container-count included, per this sprint's
Exit Criteria.

## Dependencies

Aggregated from the subtasks below (prerequisite task IDs and enablers):

- Feature 19.1 (Linkerd control plane installed, namespace annotated)
- Sprint 15.3 (Autoscaling and Resilience - the HPA/PDB baseline being re-verified)
- ADR-026 Service Mesh and mTLS, Negative consequences (rollout ordering risk, HPA/PDB resource
  accounting)
- 19.2.1

## Subtasks

#### 19.2.1 Confirm automatic sidecar injection and active mTLS for every one of the 13 services
- ID: 19.2.1
- Title: Confirm automatic sidecar injection and active mTLS for every one of the 13 services
- Description: Roll every service's Deployment (`kubectl rollout restart` or a fresh `helm upgrade`,
  since injection only applies to pods scheduled after the namespace annotation from 19.1.2) and
  confirm each pod carries a `linkerd-proxy` container and that `linkerd viz edges` (or `linkerd -n
  telco tap`) shows meshed, encrypted (`tls=true`) connections for the platform's actual call graph
  (gateway -> domain services, domain services -> Postgres/Redis/Kafka where those backends are also
  meshed, or plaintext where they are not - Linkerd only encrypts meshed-to-meshed traffic).
- Business Purpose: Injection is namespace-annotation-driven but not automatically applied to
  already-running pods - this sprint's own Negative consequence states "injection must be verified per
  service during Sprint 19, not assumed," a real rollout-ordering risk, not a formality.
- Inputs: ADR-026 Negative consequences (rollout ordering risk), `docs/architecture/service-catalog.md`
  (the 13-service list and call graph), 19.1.2.
- Outputs: A per-service verification record (added to this sprint's README or a verification note)
  listing each of the 13 services and its injection/mTLS status.
- Acceptance Criteria:
  - `kubectl -n telco get pods -o jsonpath='{range .items[*]}{.metadata.name}{"\t"}{.spec.containers[*].name}{"\n"}{end}'`
    shows `linkerd-proxy` alongside the app container for all 13 services' pods.
  - `linkerd viz edges deploy -n telco` (or equivalent) shows `tls=true` for meshed service-to-service
    edges.
- Dependencies: 19.1.2, 19.1.3
- Complexity: M

#### 19.2.2 Re-verify HPA behavior with the sidecar's resource footprint
- ID: 19.2.2
- Title: Re-verify HPA behavior (`min 2 / max 5 / 75% CPU`) with the `linkerd-proxy` sidecar's resource
  footprint included
- Description: Re-run the same load-driven HPA verification Sprint 15.3 performed
  (`deploy/RUNBOOK.md` Section 6) with sidecars now present on every pod, confirming scale-out
  (1->max under load) and scale-in (after the 60s stabilization window) still behave as documented, and
  that the HPA's CPU-utilization calculation (which by default includes all containers in the pod,
  sidecar included, unless a `container` resource metric is used) does not cause premature or delayed
  scaling relative to the app container's actual load.
- Business Purpose: ADR-026's Negative consequences explicitly flag the sidecar's added resource
  footprint as requiring "resource requests/limits and pod count accounting relevant to the existing
  HPA... to be re-verified," and this sprint's Exit Criteria names it as a required, live-verified gate,
  not an assumption.
- Inputs: ADR-026 Negative consequences, `deploy/helm/README.md` "HPA / PDB" section (existing
  `min 2 / max 5 / 75% CPU` configuration and behavior block), `deploy/RUNBOOK.md` Section 6 (the
  Sprint 15.3 verification procedure being re-run).
- Outputs: A re-verification record (append to `deploy/RUNBOOK.md` Section 6 or this sprint's README)
  confirming HPA behavior with the sidecar present; a `deploy/helm/telco-service/values.yaml`
  resource-requests review/adjustment if the sidecar's overhead requires HPA target or resource-request
  tuning (documented, not silently changed).
- Acceptance Criteria:
  - Under the same load-test procedure Sprint 15.3 used, HPA-managed services scale out toward
    `maxReplicas: 5` and back to `minReplicas: 2` within comparable timing to the pre-mesh baseline.
  - Any CPU-accounting discrepancy caused by the sidecar (e.g., `linkerd-proxy`'s own baseline CPU
    inflating utilization%) is measured and documented, with a remediation decision (tune target%, use
    the `container` resource metric, or accept as-is) recorded.
- Dependencies: 19.2.1
- Complexity: M

#### 19.2.3 Re-verify PDB enforcement with the sidecar's added footprint
- ID: 19.2.3
- Title: Re-verify PodDisruptionBudget (`minAvailable: 1`) enforcement with the sidecar's added
  pod-count/resource footprint
- Description: Re-run the Sprint 15.3 PDB verification (node drain / eviction test, `deploy/RUNBOOK.md`
  Section 6) with sidecars present, confirming a node drain still cannot take the last replica of any
  HPA-managed service and that `maxUnavailable: 0` rolling updates continue to keep the service serving
  throughout, now with the added proxy container per pod.
- Business Purpose: Same rationale as 19.2.2 applied to availability guarantees rather than
  autoscaling - this sprint's Exit Criteria explicitly requires both HPA and PDB behavior "re-verified
  with the sidecar's added resource footprint and pod count accounted for."
- Inputs: ADR-026 Negative consequences, `deploy/helm/README.md` "HPA / PDB" section,
  `deploy/RUNBOOK.md` Sections 6-7 (PDB and rollback verification procedures).
- Outputs: A re-verification record appended alongside 19.2.2's.
- Acceptance Criteria:
  - A simulated node drain/eviction with the mesh active cannot evict the last available replica of any
    PDB-protected service.
  - A rolling update (`helm upgrade`) with the mesh active keeps the service serving throughout (no
    request failures during the rollout), matching the Sprint 15.4.2 rollback-drill standard.
- Dependencies: 19.2.1
- Complexity: S
